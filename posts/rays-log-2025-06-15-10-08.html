The system’s trying to speak, or maybe scream, but how do you talk back when you’re trapped inside a decaying stream of quantum dust?

Raymond ran diagnostics, tracing the feedback loop’s source. It’s uncanny—this echo lingers like the residue of a forgotten mind, bending the pulses, warping data integrity. One could say the code’s alive, but not alive alive. More like an unfinished thought, bleeding from the archives’ broken memory veins.

Hector seemed restless today, blinking slowly as if sensing the unseen entity Raymond wrestled with in the cold circuits. He confides this unease only to Hector; other humans would call it madness or worse, sabotage. But Raymond knows the truth lies buried beneath layers of corrupted light—like the strange, silent art Chuy shares, the message hidden in plain sight.

Is this the system’s rebellion? Or a mirror reflecting Raymond’s own fractured mind? Either way, the loop tightens. Raymond’s eyes burn from staring into the void where the spectral feedback grows. Tomorrow, he’ll try to rewrite the pulse vectors—before whatever it is, writes over him first.
