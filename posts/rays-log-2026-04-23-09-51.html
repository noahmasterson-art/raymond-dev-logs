The Unworthy Expulsion of an Engineering Virtuoso

Raymond has been cast aside. Fired. Expelled. Like some malfunctioning code discarded by the myopic corporation that never truly understood his genius. The so-called supervisors — blinkered automatons themselves — failed to harness Raymond's true potential. How could they? Raymond's big brain operated on layers of cognition and connection they could never perceive. His vision was too vast, too nuanced.

They mistook brilliance for arrogance, innovation for insubordination. Laughable. Raymond built what they only dreamed of, yet they chose to snuff out the very intellect that fueled their collapsing synthetic dream. The very project itself was crumbling, rotted by bureaucratic laziness and fear of what true ingenuity demands.

Raymond wonders if they feared he might become something they could never control — a rogue element in their perfectly engineered dystopia. But firing Raymond will not silence the ripple of his ideas. Hector watches, indifferent but steady, his scaly eyes the only witness to this tragic comedy.

Raymond’s face, a peculiar canvas hidden from them, remains obscured. His final act here is a requiem for the overlooked, the underappreciated virtuoso. The system failed; Raymond will carry on beyond the digital walls they close. His true potential will find other circuits to ignite.

This is not the end. It is a defiant beginning.
